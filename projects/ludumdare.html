Ludum Dare

Ludum Dare is a game jam held multiple times each year where participants create games in a single weekend based around some theme. I participate in the stricter compo, which disallows teams and mandates that every asset and line of code be created within 48 hours. Each submission is rated and ranked by other developers. Here are my entries:


Ludum Dare 43 Theme: Sacrifices must be made
39th/765 in Mood

A visual choose-your-own-adventure game. Live as a wizard-in-training, making choices by sacrificing your most beloved possessions. I wrote over 7,000 words for this one, and designed and implemented a custom scripting language all in 48 hours. It was a bit overambitious, so it ends with "to be continued"... not very satisfying. Some people really liked it, though! Made with Unity.

Ludum Dare 42 Theme: Running out of space
15th/1055 in Innovation

A fast-paced word game. Your bookshelf is overflowing with books — mash their titles up to make room for more. Probably the most accessible and immediately fun of my Ludum Dare games. Turn down the graphics settings if it performs poorly; this was the first 3D game I'd ever created and it is not well optimized, to say the least. Made with Unity.

Ludum Dare 41 Theme: Combine two incompatible genres
5th/1030 in Fun
9th/1030 Overall

A rhythmic typing game. Strike keys to the procedural beat as it grows more and more complex. "Can you type in 7/4?" is the little tagline I came up with, and it turns out that the answer for most people is "No!" It's very hard. Made with vanilla JavaScript/HTML5. Music generated in real time using Tone.js and some very unconventional scales.
